# MIRA — Voiceover Script Sheet

All narration lines in one place for your **voice actor** or **TTS engine**. Every line is written to land in **~8 seconds** at a calm, premium pace (≈2.5 words/sec, ~16–21 words).

## Voice direction (read this first)
- **Persona:** calm, confident, premium — a trusted colleague, not a hype ad. Think Apple product film.
- **Gender:** MIRA is referred to as *"she,"* but the **narrator can be any voice** (neutral works well). If you want the narrator to *be* MIRA, use a warm female voice and keep it understated.
- **Pace:** unhurried. Let each line breathe; land the last word cleanly before the cut.
- **Tone by scene:** tension scenes = quieter, slower. Payoff scenes = warmer, a touch brighter. Never shouty.
- **Pronunciation:** "MIRA" = *MEE-rah*. "LOTO" = spell as *lock-out tag-out* if the plain acronym sounds odd. "SLA" = say the letters *S-L-A*. "PTW" = *permit to work*.
- **TTS settings starting point:** speed 0.92–0.95×, stability high, a 0.3–0.5s pause between sentences. Add commas where you want breaths.
